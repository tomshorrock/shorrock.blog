What if higher interest rates actually increase inflation?

If you’re like me, you’ll have looked at the decisions taken by central banks since the financial crisis and wonder how the ultra-low interest rates and QE never caused high inflation. In this post I’m going to take a deep dive into the relationship between monetary policy and inflation, and see if there is any opportunity for a contrarion take that results in profit in the markets.

Lets start with a look at the theory:

In general, as interest rates are reduced, more people are able to borrow more money. The result is that consumers have more money to spend, causing the economy to grow and inflation to increase. The opposite holds true for rising interest rates.
